## Problem

Krylo's existing real estate synthesis is institutionally biased — it models REITs, cap rate compression against benchmark yields, and standard DCF. Mallah operates in the structural blind spots of that model: Class C/B commercial and residential assets in secondary markets, acquired at distressed prices through local relationships and rapid cash deployment, managed with high-leverage tolerance that institutional models classify as "excessive risk" rather than "calculated alpha."

The gap is not data — it's the interpretive frame. `synthRealEstate()` would read Mallah's portfolio and flag it as overleveraged. The correct read is: the leverage IS the strategy. The management intensity IS the yield premium. The model needs a synthesizer that treats non-institutional operating patterns as alpha-generating, not as risk flags.

---

## Lens Ambiguity — Architecture Decision

Mallah operates at the intersection of two lenses:
- **REALTOR** — correct by domain (real estate transactions, occupancy, regional market)
- **INVESTOR** — correct by behavior (leverage thresholds, portfolio pressure, macro cycle timing)

**Resolution:** REALTOR lens owns this synth. INVESTOR lens co-fires via WO-1767 domain superposition when both REAL_ESTATE and INVESTMENTS score above threshold. `synthNonInstitutionalAlpha()` is the REALTOR lens synthesizer; `synthInvestor()` handles the macro overlay.

## Key Variables

### Mallah Factor (MF)
The active-management yield premium above passive institutional hold. Measures the alpha generated by:
- Aggressive repositioning of distressed assets
- Non-standard tenant relationships (local, high-churn tolerance)
- Operating velocity that institutional management structures cannot match

Formula: `MF = realized_yield - institutional_benchmark_yield`  
When MF > 0: active management is generating alpha above what a REIT would return on the same asset.  
When MF < 0: management intensity is costing more than the yield premium it generates — structural warning.

### leverage_headroom
**Shared field — WO-1778 reads this.**  
`leverage_headroom = max_serviceable_leverage - current_portfolio_leverage`  
A positive value means Mallah can absorb new acquisitions when the distress signal fires.  
A negative value means a REIT offloading signal (WO-1778 DISTRESS_ENTRY) is a risk event, not an opportunity.

This field determines whether the same COMMERCIAL_DISTRESS signal in WO-1778 is a buy trigger or a warning. Do not omit from output.

### cap_rate_spread
Delta between Mallah's realized cap rate on distressed acquisitions and the institutional floor for that asset class and region. Positive spread = arbitrage window open. Spread compressing toward zero = institutional capital entering his market — exit or reposition before compression completes.

### management_intensity_score
Proxy for the operational overhead required to sustain yield on the portfolio. High-intensity = high MF potential but high single-point-of-failure risk if management bandwidth is exceeded (the "scale-resiliency" gap in the role-play analysis).

### exit_liquidity_horizon
Estimated time before secondary market for Class C/B assets in the current regional concentration thins to illiquidity. Fed into WO-1778 for the exit risk output.

## Four Branches

### 1. isCapRate
Trigger: `cap rate|distressed|yield|arbitrage|repositioning|cash flow|acquisition`

- **CAP RATE ARBITRAGE ACTIVE** — cap_rate_spread positive, distress window open, institutional floor below Mallah's realized rate
- **DISTRESS WINDOW CLOSING** — cap_rate_spread compressing, institutional capital entering the market

### 2. isLeverage
Trigger: `leverage|debt service|interest rate|liquidity|serviceable|carry`

- **LEVERAGE HEADROOM** — leverage_headroom positive, rate environment serviceable, portfolio can absorb acquisitions
- **LEVERAGE PRESSURE** — leverage_headroom negative or declining, rate variance threatening debt service on existing assets

### 3. isRegional
Trigger: `florida|southeast|occupancy|regional|market|demand|vacancy`

- **REGIONAL DEMAND SIGNAL** — occupancy/demographic pressure in Florida/Southeast concentration; signals supply/demand shift in non-premium asset class

### 4. Default
- **NON-INSTITUTIONAL ALPHA ACTIVE** — baseline output covering MF, leverage posture, cap rate environment, exit horizon
