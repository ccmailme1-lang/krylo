# KRYLO Lens Generation Prompts (sell-grade, v2)

Paste one lens brief at a time into the prompt-taking tool, appending the STYLE BLOCK each time.
The shared block is what makes all six read as one instrument. Core principle: **dim the field,
ignite the selection in lime.**

---

## STYLE BLOCK — append to every lens prompt

> Premium financial-intelligence dashboard "KRYLO." **Pure black background (#000000).**
> **Muted, near-monochrome palette** — assign base elements across `#2d6b42` (deep green),
> `#3F7CAC` (slate-blue), `#4C9A2A` (green), `#6C5B9E` (muted purple), `#5A6072` (slate),
> `#8A9A5B` (sage). **NO orange, pink, yellow, amber, or rainbow — three hue families max.**
> **Lime `#66FF00` is reserved for the ACTIVE / HIGHLIGHTED element ONLY** — the selected flow or
> node ignites in lime while everything else stays dim / near-black. The default state is calm and
> low-contrast; interaction lights exactly one thing. IBM Plex Mono, white/light-gray labels,
> generous spacing. No gridlines, no clutter. Feel: **Bloomberg terminal meets Apple keynote** —
> minimal, dark, glowing, expensive. **Subtract, don't add. Legibility through focus.**

## 1 · SIGNAL — Situational Awareness
> Design a **network / scatter constellation** — a field of points floating on black, each a signal.
> **Point size = significance, glow/brightness = confidence**, hairline links between related signals.
> Default: the whole field is dim and even. On hover/select, the chosen signal (or domain) and its
> links **ignite lime** while the rest recede to near-black. Sparse, elegant — a star map you can
> focus. `[STYLE BLOCK]`

## 2 · FLOW — Movement Analysis
> Design a **directional-highlighting chord diagram** for six domains (Capital, Ownership, Labor,
> Media, Technology, Knowledge). Ring arcs in the muted palette. Default: a soft, low-contrast full
> ring. On selecting a domain, its **directional** in/out flows **ignite lime** and every other
> ribbon fades to near-black. Curved ribbons inside a clean ring, thin perimeter arcs. `[STYLE BLOCK]`

## 3 · PRESSURE — Constraint Analysis
> Design a **heatmap**: six domain rows × time columns. Cell intensity ramps from near-black through
> deep green, with **only the highest-pressure cells glowing lime** (lime = the hottest zone — the
> highlight). Smooth, thermal, like stress building before a fault. `[STYLE BLOCK]`

## 4 · CONVERGENCE — Evidence Corroboration
> Design a **beeswarm / force-cluster** — scattered bubbles pulling into dense cores as evidence
> corroborates. Base clusters in muted green/slate; the **strongest-converging core ignites lime**,
> weak/diffuse points stay dim. Gravitational, calm. `[STYLE BLOCK]`

## 5 · DRIFT — Temporal Evolution
> Design a **connected-scatter with fading trails** (long-exposure). Each entity leaves a soft trail
> showing trajectory, direction, acceleration over time. Trails in deep green, dimming with age; the
> **most active / accelerating path ignites lime**. `[STYLE BLOCK]`

## 6 · OPPORTUNITY — Structural Leverage
> Design a **network graph with the whole field dimmed to near-black and only the high-leverage
> structures illuminated** — bridge nodes, bottlenecks, emerging white-space **glow lime**; everything
> else recedes. The eye goes straight to where leverage forms. `[STYLE BLOCK]`

---

## Locked once, reused everywhere
When you swap test names for the six domains, assign **one fixed color per domain** from the muted
palette — that mapping carries to every lens so a domain reads the same color across the whole system.
Lime is never a domain color; it is only ever the active/highlight state.
