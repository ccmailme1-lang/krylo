## Intake Doctrine

Every concept is assumed **NOT admitted** until it proves platform leverage.

The burden of proof is on the concept.

---

## Opportunity Qualification Framework (OQF)

Instead of "scoring ideas," I'd evaluate them across five pillars.

### 1. Platform Enhancement (Mandatory)

**Question:** Does this make KRYLO objectively better?

Evidence should support an expected **≥10% improvement** in one or more areas:

* Performance
* Efficiency
* User Value
* Revenue Potential
* Marketability
* Strategic Position

Fail this gate → Reject.

---

### 2. Synergy (Mandatory)

**Question:** What existing capabilities become stronger because this exists?

Ideas that strengthen multiple existing systems receive higher value than isolated features.

Fail this gate → Reject.

---

### 3. Innovation & Differentiation

**Question:** Does this increase KRYLO's competitive advantage?

Evaluate:

* Originality
* Defensibility
* Difficulty to replicate
* Intellectual property potential
* Enterprise "wow" factor

Innovation alone is never sufficient, but meaningful differentiation increases priority.

---

### 4. Leverage

**Question:** Does this unlock future capabilities?

Examples:

* Reusable infrastructure
* New product lines
* Additional analytics
* New customer segments
* Reduced future engineering effort

High-leverage ideas compound over time.

---

### 5. Doctrine Alignment (Mandatory)

This is a binary gate.

The concept must align with KRYLO's established principles.

Questions include:

* Does it enhance rather than duplicate?
* Does it integrate with existing architecture?
* Does it preserve deterministic behavior?
* Does it maintain established authority boundaries?
* Does it avoid unnecessary complexity?

Any doctrine conflict requires explicit resolution before advancement.

---

## Advancement Decision

The output should never be just "Accepted" or "Rejected."

Instead:

* **DISCOVERY** — Continue exploration; idea is still forming.
* **CANDIDATE** — Meets admission criteria; ready for specification.
* **BACKLOG** — Approved for future implementation.
* **WORK ORDER** — Authorized for engineering.
* **REJECTED** — Does not currently meet admission standards.
* **PARKED** — Valuable concept, but not the right time.

This preserves promising ideas without letting them clutter active development.

---

### One design choice I'd make now

I would **not expose numerical scores** during intake.

Instead, I'd use structured findings for each pillar, then let the system derive an internal qualification result. This avoids debates over whether something is a "7.9 vs. 8.1" and keeps the discussion centered on *why* a concept advances or fails.

I think this is the beginning of what will become **KRYLO Development OS**—not just a backlog process, but a governance layer that determines how the platform evolves. Given how central disciplined evolution has been to KRYLO's development, I'd treat this as a first-class subsystem rather than just project management.
