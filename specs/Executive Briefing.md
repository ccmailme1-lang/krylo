Yes, Goldman Sachs heavily utilizes standardized  One-Pager  and "five-minute brief" formats to swiftly communicate complex financial analysis. These documents—whether for investment pitches, cover letters, or macroeconomic briefs—prioritize high information density, brevity, and simple aesthetics. [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]  
The strategic brief copy at Goldman Sachs generally adheres to firmwide styling and structure guidelines: [1]  
📄 Standard Formatting Guidelines 

• Typography: Firmwide style requires Arial 10-point font with simple aesthetics and clean layouts. 
• Emphasis: Limited use of colored text, boldface, all caps, italics, or underlining. 
• Length: Restricted to a single page to maintain extreme conciseness. [1, 4]  

📋 Sample Copy: The "Investment/Strategy Brief" 
While the data changes by sector, a typical Goldman-style investment strategy or company one-pager is structured to be read in just a few minutes, following this exact layout: [2, 6]  
[Company Name or Security Ticker] 

• The Bottom Line: Clear, concise 1-2 sentence statement on whether the stock is mispriced, the expected return, and the timeframe. 
• Current Market Context: Quick metrics like Current Market Cap, Revenue, and P/E ratio. [6]  

Company Overview 

• Brief 1-2 concise sentences outlining what the company does, key products/services, and target markets. [6]  

Catalysts (3-point maximum) 

• Catalyst 1: Brief description of the event, trigger, or market shift. 
• Catalyst 2: Brief explanation of how this will drive price movement. 
• Catalyst 3: Timeline of when this will occur. [6]  

Valuation & Risks 

• Valuation: DCF-implied value, current multiples, and relative context to the broader market. 
• Risks: The primary risk to the thesis compressed into 1 sentence. [6]
